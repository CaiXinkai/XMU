    Hou Z#, Cai X#, Wu D, Gong H, Wang J, Zhang Y, et al. Genome-Wide Analysis of the LEA Gene Family in Pineapple (Ananas comosus L.) Reveals Its Potential Roles in Cold Stress Response and Reproductive Development. Biology. 2025;14(1655). DOI
    Pineapple (Ananas comosus L.) is highly susceptible to cold stress, and its regulatory mechanisms during reproductive development remain poorly understood. LEA proteins are known to act as ‚Äúmolecular shields,‚Äù preventing protein aggregation and maintaining enzyme activity during dehydration or freeze‚Äìthaw cycles. To address these gaps, this study performed a comprehensive genome-wide identification, evolutionary analysis, and functional characterization of the LEA gene family in pineapple. By integrating genomic features, gene structures, regulatory network predictions, and multi-condition expression profiling, we aimed to clarify the roles of LEA genes in cold-stress responses and reproductive organ development.
    • Expansion of the LEA‚ÇÇ subfamily: The LEA‚ÇÇ subfamily is markedly expanded in pineapple, and many of its members are rapidly induced at the early stages of cold treatment, suggesting a specialized function in cold adaptation of tropical crops.
    • Identification of cold-tolerance core genes: Key cold-responsive genes, such as AcLEA32 and AcLEA33, were identified, showing stronger and faster induction in the cold-tolerant cultivar BL, making them promising targets for improving cold resistance.
    • Stage-specific peaks during floral development: Several LEA genes exhibited distinct expression peaks during pollen development and stigma maturation, indicating potential noncanonical roles in floral organ development and early embryogenesis.
    • Key regulators in the LEA network: A TF‚ÄìmiRNA‚Äìcis-element regulatory framework was constructed, highlighting ERF, BBR-BPC, and MIKC_MADS as potential master regulators, and outlining a dual regulatory pathway of LEA genes in stress response and floral development.
    This study establishes a comprehensive resource for the LEA gene family in pineapple, elucidates its evolutionary patterns and regulatory landscape, and uncovers its potential functions in cold stress response and reproductive development. These findings deepen our understanding of cold adaptation in tropical crops and provide valuable candidate genes and theoretical foundations for pineapple molecular breeding.

    Hou Z#, Cai X#, Wu J, Lu L, Liu C, Zhang Y, et al. Identification and Characterization of Hva22 Genes in Pineapple (Ananas comosus L.) Revealed Their Roles in Development Regulation and Cold Stress Response. BMC Plant Biol. 2025;25(1544). DOI
    Pineapple (Ananas comosus L.) is an economically important tropical fruit crop, but its cultivation is highly sensitive to low temperatures. This study represents the first systematic analysis of the abscisic acid (ABA) and stress-inducible HVA22 gene family in pineapple.
    • 11 members: Identified based on chromosomal location and named AcHVA22A to AcHVA22K. Subcellular localization analysis showed that most AcHVA22 proteins are located in the plasma membrane, cytoplasm, and nucleus.
    • 4 subfamilies: Phylogenetic analysis grouped all AcHVA22 genes into four distinct clades. Structural analysis indicated that members within the same clade share certain similarities. However, even within a clade, the presence of multiple regulatory factors‚Äîincluding cis-regulatory elements, transcription factors, and miRNAs‚Äîmay contribute to the functional diversity of AcHVA22 genes.
    • Abiotic stress responses: Most AcHVA22 genes responded consistently to ABA, GA, and drought treatments, whereas their responses to high and low temperatures varied.
    • Conservation across varieties: AcHVA22C, D, E, G, I, and K were upregulated under both short-term and long-term cold treatments in two different pineapple varieties, indicating their potential key roles in cold stress tolerance.
    These findings provide valuable insights for functional studies of AcHVA22 genes and offer a theoretical basis for breeding cold-tolerant pineapple varieties.

    Zheng P, Wu J, Li D, Xie S, Cai X, Xiao Q, et al. Comparative Metabolomics Analysis of Four Pineapple (Ananas comosus L. Merr) Varieties with Different Fruit Quality. Plants (Basel). 2025;25, 1544. DOI
    This study conducted a comparative metabolomic analysis of four pineapple varieties: ‚ÄúComte de Paris‚Äù (BL) and three Taiwan-introduced cultivars‚Äî‚ÄúTainong No. 11‚Äù (XS), ‚ÄúTainong No. 23‚Äù (MG), and ‚ÄúTainong No. 13‚Äù (DM). A total of 551 metabolites were identified, among which 231 (including major sugars such as sucrose, glucose, and fructose, and key acids such as citric, malic, and quinic acids) showed no significant differences among varieties, indicating that all in-season fruits can achieve balanced sugar‚Äìacid accumulation under suitable conditions. The differentially accumulated metabolites (DAMs) mainly belonged to phenolic acids, flavonoids, amino acids and their derivatives, and alkaloids, but each variety exhibited distinct metabolic preferences:
    • MG: was enriched in branched-chain amino acids and multiple flavonoids, phenolic acids, lignans, and coumarins, contributing to its unique flavor and nutritional richness.
    • XS: showed high levels of aromatic amino acids, phenolic acids, and lignans/coumarins, associated with its distinctive taste.
    • DM: exhibited dominant accumulation of jasmonic acid, potentially enhancing its adaptability to low temperatures during autumn and winter.
    • BL: contained the highest levels of L-ascorbic acid and several abundant flavonoids, offering strong antioxidant potential.
    Overall, these findings provide valuable insights for the promotion of different pineapple varieties and for guiding metabolome-based breeding and improvement programs.

    Cai X#, Li D#, Liu C#, Chen J, Wei X, Hu S, et al. Identification and characterization of GRAS genes in passion fruit (Passiflora edulis Sims) revealed their roles in development regulation and stress response. Plant Cell Reports. 2025;44(2). DOI
    GRAS genes play important roles in various biological processes, including plant growth, flower and fruit development, and responses to abiotic stresses. In this study, a comprehensive analysis of the GRAS gene family in passion fruit (Passiflora edulis Sims) was performed. The results showed that members of the DLT, PAT1, and LISCL subfamilies share similar unstructured N-terminal regions and 3D structures, which correlate with comparable tissue-specific expression patterns. Even within the same subfamily (e.g., DELLA), PeGRAS genes with different N-terminal features exhibited distinct expression profiles.
    • Gene Family: A total of 29 GRAS genes (PeGRAS1‚ÄìPeGRAS29) were identified and classified into ten subfamilies. Compared with Arabidopsis, passion fruit lacks members of the LAS subfamily but contains a rice-specific Os4-like gene (PeGRAS9).
    • Protein Structure: PeGRAS proteins possess conserved C-terminal motifs, while their N-terminal regions vary in length and sequence composition.
    • Gene Expression: Members of the PAT1 subfamily and several other PeGRAS genes are involved in stress-response pathways, with PeGRAS19 and PeGRAS26 potentially regulating cold tolerance, and PeGRAS25 and PeGRAS28 associated with drought resistance.
    • Regulatory Network: Most PeGRAS promoters exhibit a strong preference for BBR‚ÄìBPC binding motifs, and multiple miRNAs, particularly miRNA171, are predicted to regulate many of the PeGRAS genes.
    These findings provide insights into the subfamily classification, protein structure, expression patterns, and regulatory roles of GRAS genes in passion fruit and highlight candidate genes for molecular breeding aimed at improving stress tolerance.

    Lin J#, Wu J#, Zhang D#, Cai X, Du L, Lu L, et al. The GRAS gene family and its roles in pineapple (Ananas comosus L.) developmental regulation and cold tolerance. BMC Plant Biology. 2024;24(1). DOI
    Pineapple (Ananas comosus L.) is an economically important tropical fruit crop, whose growth and development are significantly affected by low temperatures. The plant-specific GRAS gene family plays crucial roles in various processes, including flower and fruit development as well as stress responses. However, the functions of the GRAS family in pineapple have not yet been systematically analyzed. In this study, 43 AcGRAS genes were identified in the pineapple genome; these genes are unevenly distributed across 19 chromosomes and 6 scaffolds and were designated AcGRAS01 to AcGRAS43 based on their chromosomal positions. Phylogenetic analysis classified these genes into 14 subfamilies: OS19, HAM-1, HAM-2, SCL4/7, LISCL, SHR, PAT1, DLT, LAS, SCR, SCL3, OS43, OS4, and DELLA.
    • Gene Structure: 60.5% of AcGRAS genes are intronless.
    • Comprehensive Expression Profiles: AcGRAS genes are primarily expressed in specific floral organs, fruit tissues, or developmental stages, indicating functional diversity during pineapple development. Moreover, most AcGRAS genes are induced by cold stress, although different members appear to play distinct roles in short-term versus long-term cold adaptation.
    • Special Expression Patterns: Notably, most members of the PAT1 subfamily are preferentially expressed during late petal development and are upregulated under cold stress, suggesting specialized roles in petal development and cold response. In contrast, no consistent expression patterns were observed among genes of other subfamilies, indicating that diverse regulatory factors‚Äîsuch as miRNAs, transcription factors, and cis-regulatory elements‚Äîmay contribute to the functional diversification of AcGRAS members, even within the same subfamily.
    In summary, this study provides the first comprehensive analysis of the GRAS gene family in pineapple, identifying 43 AcGRAS genes and revealing their diverse roles in development and stress responses. Special emphasis was placed on the PAT1 subfamily for its unique functions in petal development and cold response.

    An C#, Liao J#, Lu L#, Cai X, Liu R, Chen S, et al. From gene expression to flower patterns: genome-wide characterization of the MADS-box gene family in passion fruit (Passiflora edulis). Tropical Plants. 2024;3(1):0-. DOI
    Passion fruit (Passiflora edulis) is an economically valuable tropical fruit crop, renowned for its nutrient-rich juice, fragrant aroma, and vibrant flowers with a unique floral structure called the corona. These distinctive floral features make passion fruit an excellent candidate for studying flower organ development. MADS-box genes play crucial roles in various aspects of plant growth and development, particularly in floral morphogenesis. In this study, 52 MADS-box genes were identified in the passion fruit genome.
    • Phylogenetic Analysis The PeMADS genes were classified into two main groups: Type I (MŒ±, MŒ≤, MŒ≥) and Type II (MIKCC, MIKC*). Compared with Arabidopsis, the passion fruit genome contains significantly fewer Type I MADS-box genes, which may contribute to the reduced gene number within this family.
    • ABC(D)E Model Regulation of petals and stamens in passion fruit is similar to that in Arabidopsis, but differences were observed in the carpels and sepals. Different filiform coronas are mainly controlled by B-, C(D)-, and E-class genes, supporting the hypothesis that the filiform corona may have originated from stamens.
    • Expression Patterns PeMADS genes are involved in the development of non-floral tissues and/or in plant hormone and stress responses. The presence of multiple cis-regulatory elements in the promoters of PeMADS genes, related to growth/development, hormones, and stress responses, is likely associated with their diverse regulatory functions.
    This study provides valuable insights into the MADS-box gene family in passion fruit and their roles in floral structure development. These findings lay a foundation for the breeding of passion fruit varieties with unique ornamental traits.

    Mo W, Hong C, Gui F, Hong C, Cai X, Guan X, et al. Inhibition mechanism of water extract from spent mushroom substrate against Phytophthora infestans in potatoes. J Chin Agric Sci Technol. 2024;26(5):129‚Äì137. Link
    Spent mushroom substrate (SMS) is the residual material left after mushroom cultivation and is rich in inorganic salts and organic matter. In this study, a water extract from spent mushroom substrate (WESMS) was prepared using hot water extraction. Its UV absorption characteristics, surface charge, and particle size in water were characterized using a UV spectrophotometer and a Zeta potential/particle size analyzer. The inhibitory effect of WESMS on the pathogenic oomycete Phytophthora infestans was evaluated using the plate infiltration method and the cross-measurement diameter method. The extent of damage to pathogen cells was observed via optical microscopy and scanning electron microscopy, and agarose gel electrophoresis was used to analyze the effect of WESMS on pathogen DNA.
    • Material Characterization WESMS showed absorption in the UV-A (315‚Äì400 nm), UV-B (280‚Äì315 nm), and UV-C (100‚Äì280 nm) ranges. The particle size of WESMS in water was 3,649.27 nm.
    • Antimicrobial Test WESMS inhibited the growth of the pathogen, and the inhibitory effect increased with higher extract concentrations. At a high volume concentration of 6.25% WESMS, the inhibition rate approached 100%.
    • Mechanism Analysis After WESMS treatment, pathogen hyphae became more twisted and flattened, with significantly increased folding, indicating notable cellular damage. DNA bands from WESMS-treated samples were fainter than those in the control, and the degree of DNA damage was positively correlated with WESMS concentration.
    These results provide a scientific basis and technical support for the resourceful utilization of spent mushroom substrate and its effective control of potato late blight.

    Hou Z#, Liang J#, Cai X, Lin J, Wang X, Liu R, et al. PeHVA22 gene family in passion fruit (Passiflora edulis): initial characterization and expression profiling diversity. Frontiers in Plant Science. 2024;14. DOI
    Passion fruit (Passiflora edulis) is an economically valuable fruit crop that is highly susceptible to adverse climatic conditions. HVA22 genes are known to be ABA- and stress-inducible and play crucial roles in stress responses and growth regulation in various eukaryotes. In this study, six HVA22 genes (PeHVA22s) were identified in the passion fruit genome, all predicted to localize in the endoplasmic reticulum.
    • Phylogenetic Analysis All PeHVA22s were classified into four subgroups. Genes clustered within the same subgroup displayed relatively conserved gene structures, while PeHVA22s from different subgroups showed significant structural differences. PeHVA22A and PeHVA22C clustered closely with group II barley HVA22 genes and were induced by ABA and drought treatment, suggesting conserved functions similar to those in barley.
    • Stress Response Most PeHVA22s were upregulated after drought treatment, but their expression was suppressed under salt, low-temperature, and high-temperature conditions, indicating a unique role in drought response.
    • Tissue-Specific Expression PeHVA22 genes exhibited tissue-specific expression patterns, except for PeHVA22B, which may represent a pseudogene. Notably, PeHVA22C, PeHVA22E, and PeHVA22F were primarily expressed in fruits, suggesting involvement in fruit development. Almost all PeHVA22s showed differential expression at various stages of stamen or ovule development, implying roles in sexual reproduction in passion fruit.
    The complex functions of PeHVA22s are likely regulated by multiple factors, including transcription factors and cis-regulatory elements related to plant growth, development, hormones, and stress responses. These observations indicate that PeHVA22s may play conserved roles in ABA response and drought tolerance while participating in the regulation of growth and floral development in passion fruit.

The extraction process is simple, controllable, and scalable.     Hong C, Hong C, Mo W, Cai X, Gui F, Guan X, et al. Study on the adsorption of heavy metal nickel by biochar prepared from three kinds of bran. Chin Agric Sci Bull. 2023;39(32):40‚Äì46. DOI

    Zheng P, Shen M, Liu R, Cai X, Lin J, Wang L, et al. Revealing Further Insights into Astringent Seeds of Chinese Fir by Integrated Metabolomic and Lipidomic Analyses. Int J Mol Sci. 2023;24(20). DOI
    Chinese fir (Cunninghamia lanceolata (Lamb.) Hook.) is an important afforestation species and timber resource in southern China. However, seed abortion and a high proportion of astringent seeds significantly reduce the yield and quality of elite seeds, causing substantial economic losses. The development of astringent seeds is accompanied by marked physiological and biochemical changes. In this study, for the first time, a combined lipidomic and metabolomic analysis was conducted to systematically investigate the characteristics of astringent seeds. A total of 744 metabolites and 616 lipids were detected, among which 489 differential metabolites and 101 differential lipids were identified.
    • Metabolite Changes: Most flavonoids and tannins, as well as proline and Œ≥-aminobutyric acid (GABA), showed increased accumulation. The levels of amino acids and their derivatives, saccharides and alcohols, organic acids, and nucleotides and their derivatives were reduced.
    • Lipid Changes: Lipid unsaturation levels decreased significantly, indicating oxidative stress in the cells.
    • Lipid Subclass Changes Most lipids related to energy storage (triglycerides, diglycerides) and cell membrane composition (phosphatidic acid, phosphatidylcholine, phosphatidylethanolamine) decreased significantly. The only lipid subclass showing increased accumulation was sphingosine phosphate (SoP).
    • Hormone and Signaling Molecule Changes Indole-3-acetic acid (IAA) levels decreased, whereas salicylic acid (SA) levels increased. Both SA and SoP were closely associated with programmed cell death in astringent seeds.
    Changes in metabolites and lipids generally reflect cellular system disruption or cell death, leading to a reduction in viable cells. Based on the metabolomic and lipidomic data, this study proposes a potential model for the development of astringent seeds in Chinese fir. These findings provide new insights into the physiological mechanisms underlying astringent seeds and offer valuable biomarkers for their identification.
